Nikola Tesla

The man who gave us electricity

A multiple exposure picture of Tesla sitting next to his "magnifying transmitter" generating millions of volts.

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in the Austrian Empire (present day Croatia).
  • 1875 - Enrolled at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz on a Military Frontier scholarship.
  • 1878 - Moved to Maribor, Slovenia. Took his first job as a draftsman for a local engineering firm.
  • 1879 - Deported from Maribor for not having a residence permit.
  • 1881 - Worked at the Budapest Telephone Exchange in Hungary and Tesla was assigned the position of Chief Electrician. During his employment, Tesla made numerous improvements to the equipment and developed an amplifier device.
  • 1883 - On assignment by Continental Edison Company, Tesla was sent to Strasbourg, France to repair a new D.C. lighting system installed at the German Railway company.
  • 1884 - Arrived in New York.
  • 1885 - Investors approached Tesla and asked him to develop an improved arc lighting system, willing to finance the Tesla Electric Light Company. After the work, Tesla was forced out of the company and left with nothing.
  • 1887 - Tesla agreed to split his patents on his alternating current system with Western Union on a fifty-fifty basis in exchange for funding.
  • 1890 - Discovered wireless power.
  • 1895 - His lab caughted fire. All of his hundreds of invention models, plans, notes, laboratory data, tools, photographs, valued at $50,000, were destroyed.
  • 1896 - The new A.C. power system from Niagra Powerhouse enjoyed a flawless inauguration, transmitting electricity to Buffalo, New York 22 miles away..
  • 1899 - Tesla claimed discovery of a new geo-electrical phenomenon, which he said would allow for the transport of electricity around the world. Terrestrial stationary waves were first observed by Tesla and formed the basis for his wireless energy transfer plans and wireless communications.
  • 1906 - Announced his bladeless turbine to the world.
  • 1911 - Tested numerous models of steam turbines.
  • 1916 - Declared bankruptcy.
  • 1917 - Envisioned radar.
  • 1919 - "Electrical Experimenter" magazine began a series of articles entitled "My Inventions" by Tesla.
  • 1943 - Tesla died quietly and alone in a hotel. He was 86 years old.
